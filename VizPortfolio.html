Visualizing terrorism around the globe

Terrorists have claimed thousands of innocent lives, injured thousands of people to attain their goals. Using the database maintained by researchers at the National Consortium for the Study of Terrorism and Responses to Terrorism (START), headquartered at the University of Maryland, the visualizations attempt to provide more insight into where terrorist attacks happen, what kind of attacks or weapon are likelty to be used, what the motives are, etc.

The stories are separated into three main sections: the world, South Asia (India, Pakistan, Myanmar, Afghanistan) and North America.

The tools that are used for this project are Python, R, Tableu, Bokeh, NetworkD3, Plotly and Leaflet. All the plots are interactive.

Network D3 Madrid Bombing Network

This network depicts the individuals involved in the Madrid train bombing in 2004. The weight of the links represents how closely tied the individuals are. The further away the links are the less closely they are tied. The closest relationship is centered on trust and friendship while the furthest is based on their coparticipation in previous attacks.

DC Crime Jitters 2016

Using the data on when and what crimes are commited in District of Columbia 2016, the jitters plot was created. It shows when a crime is more likely to happen by its density. This was interesting because normally one would expect more crimes in the dark, there's more density of crimes in the afternoon. There're more crime around midnight on weekends but it's still less than afternoon. Also, the crimes in DC are mostly theft (of vehicles) and burglary, which could be the reason for afternoon's high densities.

Source: OpenData DC. Code and data

Exploring GDP Using Shiny and Plotly

Using the data from world bank, a map and a temporal plot was created to explore the GDP per capita all over the world as of 2016 as well the country of interest from 1960 to 2016. Clicking on each country on the map will cause the time series plot below to change in order to show its GDP per capita from 1960-2016. From the map we can see that the nothern America and Europe is leading in GDP per capita. Many countries also seem to have very volatile GDP per capita over the last 10 years, with US being the only country with a steady growth except for the dip in 2008.(However, the value is measured in US$ which might cause US to look steadier)

Source: World Bank. Clean data and code

Exploring how movies budget and the sex discrimination in the casts

The two plots explored different relationship between movies' budget, genres, ratings and the gender of the casts. The top plot showed how many male casts were used against the female casts, while the bottom plot shows how much a movie budget is and how they spare with critics. This can also be further explored based on different genres. The relationship between budget and voting average seems to follow a normal distribution, which means even the movies with big budget can end up with pretty mediocre reviews. By further delving into each genres, this does not hold for all of them. And there's also an obvious tendency for having more male casts than female casts overall, which seems to be consistent through all genres.

Source: this Kaggle repo. Clean data and Code

Capital Bikeshare Ridership in 2016 Explored on Shiny App

The stacked area graph explored how Capital Bikeshare ridership differs over time and days. Different options of membership also shows the differing usages between the two types of members. US Health Measures

The two dashboards are created to convey how data can be misleading. The two dashboards differ by county level and state level only. Some of the states which seem very healthy on a state level sometimes have some of the least healthy counties, for example Texas. In this cases, while it might be very important to focus funding and other resources to these less healthy communities, the resources could be mis-distributed if the overall state level is looked at.
